Step-by-Step Guide

Host on GitHub Pages — Free Forever

No coding knowledge needed. Follow these 10 steps exactly and your website will be live at https://prashantcto.github.io within 5 minutes.

01
Create GitHub Account
Go to github.com and click "Sign up". Enter your email, create a password, and choose a username. Use prashantcto as your username — this becomes part of your website URL. Verify your email to activate the account.
02
Create a New Repository
After logging in, click the "+" icon (top right) → "New repository". In the Repository name field type exactly: prashantcto.github.io (must match your username). Set it to Public. Do NOT tick any other options. Click "Create repository".
03
Upload Your HTML File
On the repository page, click "uploading an existing file" (blue link in the middle of the page). Drag and drop your index.html file into the upload area, or click "choose your files" to browse. Wait for the upload to complete — you'll see the filename appear.
04
Commit the File
Scroll down below the upload box. You'll see a "Commit changes" section. In the first text box (commit message) type: Add website. Leave everything else as default. Click the green "Commit changes" button. Your file is now saved to GitHub.
05
Enable GitHub Pages
Click Settings (tab at the top of your repository, with a gear icon). Scroll down the left sidebar and click Pages. Under "Source", click the dropdown that says "None" and select "main" branch. Click Save.
06
Wait for Deployment
After clicking Save, GitHub will show a message: "Your site is being published" with an orange dot. Wait 1–3 minutes and refresh the page. When the dot turns green and shows "Your site is published at https://prashantcto.github.io" — you're live!
07
Visit Your Live Website
Open a new browser tab and go to: https://prashantcto.github.io. Your CDO/CTO website is now live on the internet, accessible by anyone worldwide. Copy this URL and add it to your LinkedIn profile under "Website" and in your email signature.
08
Add URL to LinkedIn
On LinkedIn → Edit profile → scroll to "Contact info" → click the pencil → add your website URL https://prashantcto.github.io under "Website". Set the label as "Portfolio" or "Personal Website". Recruiters clicking your LinkedIn profile will go directly to your site.
09
Update the Website
Whenever you want to update the site: go back to your GitHub repo → click on index.html → click the pencil icon (Edit) → paste updated HTML → click "Commit changes". Your live site updates automatically within 1–2 minutes. No re-uploading needed.
10
Optional: Custom Domain
Buy prashanttripathi.in (~₹800/yr) on GoDaddy. In GitHub Pages settings, type your domain in the "Custom domain" box and save. Then in GoDaddy DNS settings, add a CNAME record pointing to prashantcto.github.io. Your site then loads at your own domain — far more impressive for CDO/CTO positioning.

Quick summary: github.com → Sign up (username: prashantcto) → New repo named prashantcto.github.io → Upload index.html → Commit → Settings → Pages → Enable main branch → Done. Total time: under 5 minutes.
